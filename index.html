🌡️ Temperature Zone Guide
🧊 Cold · Under 60°F▼
What's happening
Cell slows or shuts off below 55°F. Cold water becomes corrosive — leaches calcium from your shell and plates. Biggest risk is a negative LSI going unnoticed over winter.
ParameterCold-Water Tip
Salt CellExpect low/no output below 55°F.
CalciumKeep at 280–350 ppm to protect shell.
LSI Target0.0 to +0.3 — slightly scale-forming protects surfaces.
Action Plan
Before temps drop below 60°F: run full panel, bump calcium to 280–350, set pH 7.4–7.6, run LSI. If negative, correct before cell shuts off.
🌤️ Mild · 60–75°F▼
What's happening
Easiest chemistry window. Cell runs normally. This is your window to dial in baseline chemistry before summer demand spikes.
ParameterTip
Cell Output50–70% usually enough.
CYAGet to 70–80 ppm before UV season.
LSI Target0.0 to +0.2.
☀️ Warm · 76–85°F▼
What's happening
Chlorine demand rises, pH drifts up faster from electrolysis. Calcium starts dropping out of solution. Early tile scale and cell fouling begin here.
ParameterTip
Free ChlorineTest every other day. Bump cell to 75–85%.
pHDrifts toward 8.0. Add acid every 2–3 days.
LSI Target0.0 to +0.3.
🔥 Hot · 86°F+▼
What's happening
Chlorine demand can double. Algae thrives. Calcium scaling is aggressive — hot water makes CaCO₃ dramatically less soluble. Peak tile and cell fouling season.
ParameterTip
Free ChlorineTest daily. Cell at 90–100%. Shock after every party.
pHCan spike to 8.2+ overnight. Add acid every 3–4 days.
CalciumKeep at 200–250 ppm to reduce scaling.
LSI Target-0.1 to +0.2 to offset heat-scaling.
🪨 Tile Scaling: What to Do
Stage 1 · Light Haze▼
What it looks like
Thin white film at waterline. Chalky feel. Wipes off partially with wet cloth.

✅ DIY Fix — Easy

Step 1: Lower pH to 7.2 for 48–72 hrs — slightly acidic water dissolves light scale.

Step 2: Scrub with nylon brush and tile calcium remover (Jack's Magic, BioGuard Off the Wall).

Step 3: Add sequestering agent monthly (Natural Chemistry Scale Free).

Step 4: Run LSI. If above +0.3, lower pH or calcium.

Stage 2 · Moderate Buildup▼
What it looks like
Visible white/tan crusty deposits. Rough. Won't wipe off. May extend above and below waterline.

⚠️ DIY with Effort

Step 1: Lower water below tile line to work on dry surface.

Step 2: Apply pool-safe calcium remover paste. Wait 5–10 min. Scrub with stiff nylon brush or pumice stone (test pumice first).

Step 3: Rinse, refill, rebalance water, add sequestering agent.

Step 4: Hold calcium at 200–250 and pH at 7.2–7.4 for the rest of the season.

Stage 3 · Heavy Crust▼
What it looks like
Thick hard deposits, barnacle-like. Brown or white chunks. Covers grout lines. Multiple seasons of buildup.

🛑 Call a Pro

Hire a tile cleaning service for bead blasting. While waiting: run LSI under +0.2, lower pH to 7.2, lower calcium to 200–250, add sequestering agent weekly. Inspect cell plates — if tile is this scaled, the cell likely is too.

Saltwater Pool Guide
💧 Free Chlorine▼

FC is your active sanitizer. Salt cell generates it. Use FAS-DPD (Taylor K-2006) for accurate readings.

LevelStatusAction
<1 ppmLowIncrease cell output or shock
1–3 ppmIdealMaintain
3–5 ppmHighReduce cell output
>5 ppmExcessReduce output, test daily

Salt water drifts upward from cell electrolysis. Keep 7.2–7.6.

Protects FC from UV. Salt pools need 70–80 ppm. Too high = chlorine lock.

Most cells best at 2700–3400 ppm. Verify with Taylor kit — cell sensors drift.

📅 Maintenance Schedule▼

2–3× per week: Test FC and pH.

Weekly: Full panel. Clean cell if output dropping.

Monthly: Visual cell check. CYA test. Sequestering agent.

Seasonally: Clean filter. Run LSI before opening/closing.

After rain or parties: Test immediately. Shock if needed. Cell at 100% for 24–48 hrs.
